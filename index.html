Portfolio Analysis

📊 Most Popular Positions

PFFA leads with 12 member holdings, followed by a cluster of tickers with 9–10 holdings each: KGLD, QQQI, FSCO, CEFS, BTCI, and ASGI. The top 10 positions collectively reflect strong community preference for income-focused vehicles across a variety of structures — ETFs, CEFs, and BDCs.

🏗️ Dominant Asset Classes

BDCs are the most represented category by count, with 17+ tickers including ARCC, MAIN, CSWC, HTGC, and BXSL. Covered Call ETFs — particularly the NEOS suite — are also widely held, reflecting community interest in option-income strategies layered on major indices and assets.

📈 High-Yield Positions

Several tickers carry exceptional yields: MSTY at 105.25%, BLOX at 36%, KSLV at 27.54%, QDTE at 27.26%, ULTY at 65.43%, and CHPY at 45.22%. These are primarily YieldMax and option-overlay products tied to volatile underlying assets like Bitcoin and semiconductors.

Note: Elevated distribution yields often reflect return of capital or options premium capture, not organic income growth. These require closer individual evaluation.

🔍 Diversification Observations

The community shows healthy spread across credit (PIMCO CEFs), infrastructure (UTF, ASGI), real estate (RLTY, IYRI), CLOs (OXLC, ECC), and international equity (IDVO, NIHI). A handful of members appear to concentrate in high-income crypto-linked products, which is worth monitoring for risk exposure.

⚖️ Yield ≠ Quality

As always, a higher yield does not automatically imply a better or safer investment. Distribution sustainability, NAV erosion, leverage levels, and tax treatment vary significantly across these structures. Members are encouraged to research individual holdings thoroughly before acting on any information discussed in the community.

💡 Allocation Signals

Average allocations across most tickers are modest, typically under 4%, suggesting members generally practice position-sizing discipline. JAAA stands out with the highest average allocation at 7.60%, reflecting member confidence in its lower-risk AAA CLO structure as a core holding.
