Portfolio Analysis

📊 Most Popular Positions

PFFA leads with 15 member holdings, followed by FSCO at 13. A strong cluster of tickers sit at 10–11 holdings each: QQQI, KGLD, CEFS, BTCI, NIHI, ASGI, and ADX. The top 10 collectively reflect strong community preference for income-focused vehicles across ETFs, CEFs, and BDCs.

🏗️ Dominant Asset Classes

BDCs remain the most represented category by count, with tickers including ARCC, MAIN, CSWC, HTGC, and BXSL. Covered Call ETFs — particularly the NEOS suite — are widely held, reflecting community interest in option-income strategies layered on major indices, assets, and international equities.

📈 High-Yield Positions

Several tickers carry exceptional yields: MSTY at 68.45%, ULTY at 65.90%, CHPY at 45.62%, BLOX at 36%, KSLV at 27.54%, and QDTE at 28.57%. These are primarily YieldMax and option-overlay products tied to volatile underlying assets like Bitcoin and semiconductors.

Note: Elevated distribution yields often reflect return of capital or options premium capture, not organic income growth. These require closer individual evaluation.

🔍 Diversification Observations

The community shows healthy spread across credit (PIMCO CEFs), infrastructure (UTF, ASGI), real estate (RLTY, IYRI), CLOs (OXLC, ECC), and international equity (IDVO, NIHI). New additions such as AGNC and GPIQ highlight growing interest in mortgage REITs and Nasdaq-100 premium income products.

⚖️ Yield ≠ Quality

As always, a higher yield does not automatically imply a better or safer investment. Distribution sustainability, NAV erosion, leverage levels, and tax treatment vary significantly across these structures. Members are encouraged to research individual holdings thoroughly before acting on any information discussed in the community.

💡 Allocation Signals

Average allocations remain modest for most tickers, typically under 4%, suggesting members generally practice position-sizing discipline. GPIQ stands out with the highest average allocation at 6.42%, with GPIX and QQQI also above 6%, reflecting member confidence in broad-market and Nasdaq covered call strategies as core holdings.
